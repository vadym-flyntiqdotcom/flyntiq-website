This Cookie Policy explains how Flyntiq uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on flyntiq.com. By continuing to use our website, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy.

1. What are cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, to remember your preferences, and to provide information to website owners about how visitors use their site.

Cookies can be:

  • Session cookies — temporary cookies that are deleted when you close your browser
  • Persistent cookies — cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them
  • First-party cookies — set directly by our website
  • Third-party cookies — set by third-party services we use (e.g. analytics tools)

2. Cookies we use

Strictly necessary cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to function properly. They cannot be disabled. They do not store any personally identifiable information.

  • Session management — keeps track of your session as you navigate the site
  • Security cookies — help protect against cross-site request forgery attacks

Analytics cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting anonymous information. This helps us improve our website and services.

  • Google Analytics (_ga, _gid, _gat) — tracks page views, session duration, and user behaviour. Data is anonymised and aggregated. Retained for 14 months.

You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking at any time by visiting tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout.

Functional cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalisation. They may be set by us or by third-party providers.

  • Preference cookies — remember your settings and preferences on return visits

Marketing cookies

We do not currently use marketing or advertising cookies on this website. We do not serve ads on flyntiq.com.

3. Third-party cookies

The following third-party services may set cookies when you interact with elements on our website:

  • Calendly — when you interact with our booking widget, Calendly may set cookies to manage your scheduling session. See Calendly's Privacy Policy.
  • Google Fonts — loads our website fonts from Google's servers. Google may set performance cookies. See Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Google Analytics — as described above. See Google's Privacy Policy.

We do not control third-party cookies. Please refer to the respective third-party privacy policies for more information.

4. Managing your cookie preferences

You have several options to manage or disable cookies:

Browser settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically:

  • View cookies stored on your device
  • Delete all or specific cookies
  • Block cookies from specific websites
  • Block all third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies (note: this may break some website functionality)

Instructions for managing cookies in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Opt-out tools

  • Google Analytics opt-out: tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout
  • Your Online Choices (EU): youronlinechoices.com

Please note: Disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website. Strictly necessary cookies cannot be disabled as they are essential for the site to work.
